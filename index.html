Diverse artist representation has long been an issue in art museums, but has this changed in our contemporary times?

Take a look at artist representation data from one of the most well-recognized collections of modern art: The Museum of Modern Art (MoMA).

MoMA began acquiring artworks in 1929 at its establishment.
We are nearing a century of acquisition data, but what does it show about gender representation in the arts?

As a museum that prides itself on making strides in gender equity, do you think there has been a significant increase in the number of female pieces acquired over the last hundred years?

While women artists remain marginalized in the museum collection, we should celebrate the pioneers and explore the diversity one can find within the collection today.


Especially after Linda Nochlin's seminal essay, "Why Have There Been No Great Women Artists" was released in 1971, people today agree that women artists are in a much better position today than they were decades ago.

With more recent increased awareness of the inequity in all fields, there has been much pressure to both increase representation and herald the women artists already found in museums. These artists are leading the way for many.
